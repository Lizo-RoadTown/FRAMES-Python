## Why This Exists (The Real Story)

### The Problem You've Probably Experienced

You join an engineering team. Everyone's busy. You want to help, but:

- **You don't know what you don't know.** Where do you even start?
- **Documentation is scattered.** Some in Notion, some in Google Docs, some in someone's head.
- **Mentors are swamped.** They want to help but don't have time to explain everything from scratch.
- **You feel behind.** Everyone else seems to know what they're doing.

So you spend weeks feeling lost, asking the same questions others asked last semester, and not actually building anything.

### The Solution: Structured Onboarding

Ascent Basecamp fixes this by:

1. **Giving you a clear path.** Modules are organized by subsystem (Power, Structures, Avionics, etc.) and difficulty level.
2. **Teaching you what your team actually uses.** No generic "Intro to Arduino" courses. This is "How We Flash Firmware on Our Flight Computer."
3. **Letting you learn at your own pace.** Mobile-friendly, works on your phone, saves your progress.
4. **Making knowledge reusable.** When you graduate, your learning doesn't disappear. Future students build on what you learned.

## Special Feature: Race Mode (Optional)

Want to make learning competitive? Enable **Race Mode**:

### What It Is
You race against **"ghost cohorts"**â€”students from previous semesters who completed the same modules.

## FAQ

### Do I have to complete modules in order?
**Mostly, yes.** Some modules have **prerequisites** (you need to finish Module A before Module B). This ensures you have foundational knowledge before tackling advanced topics.

But you can usually work on multiple subsystems at once (e.g., Power + Avionics).

---

### What if I already know some of this?
**Skip ahead!** If you're already familiar with a topic, you can:
- Skim the reading sections
- Jump to the quiz/exercise to confirm you know it
- Mark it complete and move on

The system tracks what you've done, so you get credit.

---

### Are modules graded?
**No.** This is not a class. There are no grades, no tests, no failure.

The quizzes and checks are **for you** to confirm you understand. If you get something wrong, you see hints and try again. That's it.

---

### Can I do modules on my phone?
**Yes!** Ascent Basecamp is a **Progressive Web App (PWA)**, which means:
- Works on mobile browsers (Chrome, Safari)
- Responsive design (adjusts to screen size)
- Can install as an app icon on your home screen
- Works offline (once you've loaded a module)

Perfect for learning during commutes, breaks, or whenever you have spare time.

---

### What if I get stuck?
**Multiple layers of support:**

1. **Hints in the module** (click "Show Hint" for progressive guidance)
2. **External resources** (links to datasheets, documentation, videos)
3. **Ask your mentor** (module shows who created itâ€”reach out to them)
4. **Team lead dashboard** (they can see where you're stuck and offer help)

---

### How long does it take to complete a subsystem?
**Depends on the subsystem and your background.**

Typical estimates:
- **Orientation level:** 2-4 hours (basic understanding)
- **Competency level:** 10-20 hours (can work independently)
- **Integration level:** 5-10 hours (understand cross-team dependencies)
- **Autonomy level:** 10-15 hours (leadership/advanced skills)

**Total per subsystem:** 25-50 hours

**But remember:** You don't have to finish everything. Focus on what your team needs you to know.

---

### Can I revisit modules later?
**Absolutely!** Ascent Basecamp is designed as a **reference library**.

- Forgot how to flash firmware? Go back to that module.
- Need to double-check a procedure? All modules stay accessible.
- Want to refresh before a test/review? Use it as study material.

The system tracks "revisits" separately from first completions, so you can return anytime.

## Tips for Success

### 1. Do a Little Every Day
**Better:** 30 minutes/day for 5 days (2.5 hours total)
**Worse:** 5 hours in one marathon session (burnout + poor retention)

Small, consistent progress > cramming.

---

### 2. Actually Do the Exercises
**Don't skip hands-on sections.** Reading â‰  learning.

- If a module says "Flash firmware to a board," do it.
- If it says "Calculate a power budget," actually calculate it.
- If it says "Run this command," run it and see what happens.

---

### 3. Use Race Mode Strategically
- **Turn it ON** if you need motivation/focus
- **Turn it OFF** if it stresses you out

There's no shame in going at your own pace.

---

### 4. Revisit When Needed
If you complete a module but forget the details weeks later:
- **Go back and skim it.** It's faster the second time.
- **Treat it as a reference manual,** not just a one-time course.

---

### 5. Give Feedback
See a typo? Confusing section? Outdated procedure?

- Tell your team lead
- Or leave a comment (if the system supports it)

Your feedback makes modules better for future students.
